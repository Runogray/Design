Meet Wyre Smart Energy System

The Wyre Smart Energy Billing and Management Platform is specifically designed for estates and sub-letting facilities in Nigeria. It empowers users to actively manage and optimize their energy consumption while offering seamless online energy unit purchases for enhanced customer convenience. The platform provides greater transparency and control for facility managers and owners, streamlining operations and financial management. Additionally, it promotes energy-efficient behaviors through insightful analytics and personalized recommendations.

Comprehensive Energy Data Management for Wyre

Facility managers at estates and sub-letting facilities required a robust system to manage real-time and historical energy consumption data. The goal was to design a comprehensive solution that would store detailed energy data and present it in an easily understandable format, enabling informed decision-making and optimized energy distribution.

Problem Statement

Estates and sub-letting facilities in Nigeria face challenges in managing and optimizing energy consumption. Existing methods lack transparency and efficiency, resulting in high operational costs and customer dissatisfaction. Facility managers need a solution to streamline energy management, financial processes, and promote energy-efficient behaviors. The Wyre Smart Energy Billing and Management Platform addresses these issues by providing active energy management, seamless online energy unit purchases, and insightful analytics, enhancing operational control and customer convenience.

Design Process

Wyre dashboard is made up of a comprehensive energy data management system, which included a well-designed database schema for storing energy consumption data and user-friendly data visualization components. This solution provided facility managers with the tools they needed to efficiently monitor and optimize energy usage.

User Research

We conducted interviews and surveys with facility managers to understand their needs and pain points. Key insights included:

  • A need for detailed and easily accessible energy consumption data.
  • The importance of visualizing trends over time to identify inefficiencies.
  • A desire for user-friendly interfaces that facilitate quick decision-making.

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed existing energy management systems to identify best practices and areas for improvement. This helped us determine the most effective ways to present data and the essential features needed.

User Profile Information Architecture

Key Components

Develop intuitive data visualization components to present energy consumption trends and distribution patterns.

Real-Time Monitoring Dashboard:

  • Live Data Feeds: Display real-time energy consumption data using line graphs and bar charts.
  • Interactive Elements: Allow users to filter data by time periods, energy sources, and specific facilities.

Historical Data Analysis:

  • Trend Analysis: Use line charts and area graphs to show energy consumption trends over various time periods (daily, weekly, monthly).
  • Comparative Analysis: Enable comparisons between different time periods or facilities to identify patterns and anomalies.
